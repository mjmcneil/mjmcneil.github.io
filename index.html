Hello there! I’m a Systems Design Engineer currently based in Montreal, Canada. Professionally, I’m fascinated by how society interacts with technology and how that affects user experience methods. I’m interested in professional opportunities including data analytics, UI/UX, and product development.

Education

I graduated as a Systems Design Engineer from the University of Waterloo in 2014. Now you’re probably wondering, “What is Systems Design?”. Trust me you aren’t the first to have this question! Systems Design is an interdisciplinary engineering program where students study many different aspects of engineering and then apply these core concepts in developing design projects. Within my design groups I worked on a wide spectrum of projects ranging from an automated hospital triage system, self-heating gloves, and building furniture optimized for moving. In my final years, I took courses specializing in interface and user experience design methodology.

I was also very active outside of the classroom. Most notably I was a member of the varsity swim team for three years and was elected the Vice-President Education for the Engineering Society.

One of my favourite parts of University was volunteering as an orientation leader. I was leader for all four years of my undergrad and an Aerial Photo director for three years. This event involved arranging 2000 students into our expertly calculated design and handling all aspects of the coordination of the event.

Professional Experience

Currently I’m working as a front-end developer and designer at Ciena Canada. My team works to develop custom application for clients that leverage unique data from our customers network. Our web application framework enables us to display this content from an aggregated database using a REST API framework. I both design and develop the front-end of the web application primariyly using JavaScript, CSS3, HTML5, and the DOJO widget based component library.

In addition to a high-academic standard, Waterloo Engineering is well-known for their world-class Cooperative Education System. During my five years of study, my program alternated between studying and coop even four months. In total I had six different coop jobs at a wide range of companies, each exposing me to a different work environment and offering a real-life application process. I had the opportunity to observe many different industries including financial, non-profit, telecommunications, and product development.
